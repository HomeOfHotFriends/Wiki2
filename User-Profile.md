## Whakapapa

The first real engagement happened at 15 with Catch-22 — not because it was understood, but because it was *felt*. The paranoia of the book was structural, woven into the writing itself, separate from plot or character. That capacity to represent an experience through form, not just content, left a mark that never fully faded.

This became a seven-year deep-dive into literary analysis: postmodern literature via a generous librarian, Wikipedia rabbit holes, and eventually a move to Dunedin for an English Literature and Gender Studies degree. The continental philosophy available there was mostly filtered through Gender Studies — Foucault first, then the encounter with Deleuze and Guattari's *Anti-Oedipus* at 20.

## The Anti-Oedipus Encounter

> *Still one of the most fun reading experiences of my life.*

The first reading of *Anti-Oedipus* landed like Catch-22 had: overwhelming, thrilling, and largely unresolved. Something was clearly happening in it — desiring-machines, flows, the rhizome — but the full map wasn't yet visible.

The philosophical framework mapped onto a period of depression and produced a distorted reading: Anti-Oedipus as nihilism, all meaning as fascism, all certainty as oppression. Years of dismantling every scaffold without building anything in its place.

The turn came through the concept of **dislodging**: instead of opposing a thing, feeding the flows that make it irrelevant. Not pushing against the rock in the stream — feeding the stream until the rock moves on its own.

> *"There is no truth that does not 'falsify' established ideas and opinions. The true is not the real or the apparent but the production of the new."*
> — Deleuze, *Difference and Repetition*

From this: truth is not discovered but produced. Beliefs can be *chosen* — not as arbitrary relativism, but as creative selection. This is the philosophical whakapapa of the entire **j** project.

## Musical Genealogy

Music started at 15: solo bass and vocals, alt-rock aesthetics, writing like Stephen Malkmus and delivering like Lou Reed. The goal was to say nothing in many different ways.

Sadness opened up more direct expression. A band formed — members met in supermarkets, bathrooms, streets. Emo arrived: Dads, Places to Hide, Cap'n Jazz, Saetia, Orchid. Screaming stopped being painful and started being precise.

One day of journalled writing — the worst day of depression yet, pencil and paper on a deck — became the lyric source for an entire power-violence band. That writing didn't try to hide meaning. It was plain and straight to the point because it didn't need to hide anymore.

The lyrical philosophy that emerged: lyrics are least important as literal meaning; word choice matters for how letters and syllable weights add to instrumental expression. The hook matters more than the verse. Simplicity over display.

## The Law

> *There is one law that I know. Only one I can state as truth.*  
> *It is my most precious stone and I present it as **taonga**.*

**Extreme depth of the whole achieved through extreme simplicity of the parts.**

This law emerged from shmup design — from EspRaDe and DonPachi, from enemy waves that are each individually simple but combinatorially deep. It maps exactly onto what Deleuze means by production: the simple part does not contain the whole, but produces it through connection.

Every creative and technical decision in **j** runs through this law.
