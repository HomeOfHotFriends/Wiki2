Page 1: The Beauty of Limits

In lines and grids, our freedom finds its form,
Constraint, the mother of invention's spark.
We rhyme in bytes, in cycles, in the norm,
And find the infinite inside the mark.
Te Reo: "Raina" = line, "here" = boundary

Page 2: Pooh and the Algorithm

"Oh bother," said Pooh, "my code will not run!"
Piglet peered in, "Have you tried line one?"
Eeyore just sighed, "It’s all quite absurd."
But Tigger bounced in and refactored a word.
Te Reo: "Kupu" = word, "hoa" = friend

Page 6: Simplicity of Parts

Extreme depth of the whole
  through extreme simplicity
    of the parts.
Whakapapa roots, not gospel—
  a tree of code, a law of light.
Te Reo: "whakapapa" = genealogy, "kōwae" = module

Page 7: The Law

There is one law that I know.
Only one I can state as truth.
It is my most precious stone
and I present it as taonga.

    Extreme depth of the whole
    achieved through extreme
    simplicity of the parts.
Te Reo: "taonga" = treasure
